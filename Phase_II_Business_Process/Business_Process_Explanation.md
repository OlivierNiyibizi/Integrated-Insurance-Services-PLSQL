## Integrated Insurance Services Project

### 1. Process Scope
The business process modeled in this phase focuses on the core insurance operations of an insurance company. The system manages customer registration, policy subscription, premium payment processing, policy activation, policy expiration, and insurance claim handling. The scope covers the interaction between customers, insurance officers, and the Oracle-based information system.

### 2. Objectives
- Automate insurance policy management
- Reduce manual paperwork and errors
- Ensure accurate premium and claim processing
- Provide real-time access to insurance data
- Support management decision-making through analytics

### 3. Key Actors and Roles
**Customer**
- Registers personal details
- Selects insurance policies
- Makes premium payments
- Submits insurance claims

**Insurance Officer**
- Verifies customer information
- Approves policy activation
- Reviews and processes claims
- Manages policy lifecycle

**Insurance System (Oracle Database)**
- Stores and validates all data
- Automatically updates policy status
- Enforces business rules
- Generates audit logs and reports

### 4. Business Process Flow
1. Customer registers in the system
2. Customer selects an insurance policy
3. System calculates premium
4. Customer makes payment
5. System activates policy
6. Policy status is monitored automatically
7. Customer submits claim
8. Claim is approved or rejected
9. System records all activities for auditing

### 5. MIS Relevance
The system supports Management Information Systems by:
- Providing accurate transactional data
- Enabling performance monitoring
- Supporting operational and strategic decisions
- Improving efficiency and transparency

### 6. Analytics Opportunities
- Analysis of premium revenue trends
- Active vs expired policies
- Claim approval rates
- Customer policy history
